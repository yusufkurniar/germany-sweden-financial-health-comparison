# 📊 Corporate Financial Resilience During COVID-19

Comparative analysis of firm-level financial adjustment in Germany and Sweden during the COVID-19 period using data from 2018 to 2021. This project evaluates how firms adapted profitability, leverage, liquidity, and structural financial relationships under pandemic-related economic stress.

## 🌐 Live Website

🔗 https://yusufkurniar.github.io/germany-sweden-financial-health-comparison/

## 📖 Project Overview

The website contains two analytical reports.

### Report 1: Financial Health of Germany Firms During COVID-19

Examines how German firms responded to the COVID-19 shock. The analysis focuses on profitability dispersion, leverage changes, liquidity preservation, and sectoral divergence between pre-pandemic and pandemic periods. Findings indicate stable median profitability but wider firm-level dispersion, suggesting uneven impact rather than systemic financial collapse.

### Report 2: Financial Health of Firms During COVID-19: Germany and Sweden

Extends the analysis to a cross-country comparison. Evaluates differences in balance-sheet strategies, leverage sensitivity, liquidity buffers, and shifts in the liquidity and profitability relationship during 2020 and 2021. Results highlight coordinated balance-sheet responses in Germany and greater heterogeneity across firms in Sweden.

## 🔍 Key Insights

• Median profitability remained broadly stable in both countries  
• Firm-level dispersion increased during the pandemic  
• Liquidity buffers were preserved across industries  
• Leverage rose modestly, with slightly higher debt reliance in Germany  
• Swedish firms displayed greater cross-firm heterogeneity
